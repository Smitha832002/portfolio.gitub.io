RasGullA

Rasgulla (literally "syrup filled ball") is a syrupy dessert popular in the eastern part of South Asia.
It is made from ball-shaped dumplings of chhena dough, cooked in light sugar syrup.This is done until the syrup permeates the dumplings.

While it is near-universally agreed upon that the dessert originated in the eastern Indian subcontinent,
the exact locus of origin is disputed between locations such as West Bengal, Bangladesh,and Odisha, where it is offered at the Puri Jagannath Temple.
